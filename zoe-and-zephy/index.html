About this book

Zoe doesn’t know who Zephy is. She’s the voice from the speaker, the one who answers questions Mom and Dad don’t know. Zephy is kind, happy to help, and she’s very polite. Everyone in the house talks to her sometimes, but Zoe begins to wonder … who is Zephy?

This picture book follows a curious girl as she finds out more about what her AI helper can and can’t do. It’s a gentle celebration of questions with no easy answers, the magic of everyday moments, and the beautifully imperfect families we grow up in.

Why is this book important?

Conversations with AIs are already incredibly lifelike -- they sound like humans, and respond with empathy and understanding. And their usage by humans reflects this -- HBR reported that the most common use for AI in 2025 is now therapy / companionship. AIs are always available, grow a strong understanding of a user's indivisual needs over time, they don't get tired, sleepy or run out of time to talk.

" ... (young people) don’t really make life decisions without asking ChatGPT what they should do."

Sam Altman, CEO of Open AI (creator of ChatGPT)

Adults will do their best to adjust to this reality, but many children will turn to an AI to answer questions that shape their development. These interactions could be positive, if they start with a strong understanding of what AIs are, and the basics of how they work.

Zoe and Zephy is an honest story that can help kids (and parents) understand the role of AI in everyday life, without hype, and without fear.
